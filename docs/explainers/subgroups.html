The subgroup fishing expedition

The topline on a short-video app experiment is flat, so someone scans the country breakdown and finds the effect is "significant in Brazil." Here is how often Brazil shows up when the true effect is zero everywhere.

4,000 simulated experiments, 20 segments each, every segment truly null. Reproduce this figure.

The temptation

Twenty country segments sit in the dashboard. The topline is flat, the quarter is ending, and each segment has its own p-value. Scanning them feels like diligence. It is twenty chances at the same 5% mistake.

Someone is always significant

Scanning all 20 segments finds at least one "significant" subgroup in 63.8% of these null experiments, and on average produces 1.0 false subgroup per experiment. Whichever country it lands on gets the narrative: "the feature works for Brazil."

It is multiple comparisons in a trench coat

The arithmetic is the family-wise error rate: with 20 independent null segments, the chance that at least one clears 0.05 is 1 - 0.9520 = 64.2%. The simulation lands on top of the formula. Nothing about segments makes the problem special; it is many tests wearing one experiment's credibility.

Pre-register or correct

Name the decision-relevant segment before launch and its false positive rate stays at 5.0%. Scan the rest as exploratory with a Benjamini-Hochberg correction and the family stays at 5.2%, the nominal level. A subgroup found by fishing is a hypothesis for the next experiment, not a launch argument for this one.

The memo you write instead

Recommendation

Do not launch on the Brazil segment. A post-hoc scan of 20 segments finds at least one false "significant" subgroup in 63.8% of null experiments (measured over 4,000 simulated runs), which makes this finding indistinguishable from noise. Re-run with Brazil pre-registered as the primary segment if the hypothesis is worth traffic.

Method note: Benjamini and Hochberg, "Controlling the False Discovery Rate", JRSS-B 57(1), 1995; the implementation is validated against statsmodels in tests/unit/test_corrections.py.
